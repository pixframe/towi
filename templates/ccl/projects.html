A videogame for the neuropsychological screening of children

Published

  • Description: In 2016, a research was carried out to validate the relationship between the results of traditional neuropsychological evaluations and Towi's skills assessment. According to the research, there is a significant correlation between most of the metrics obtained with standardized tests and those obtained with the Towi evaluation. The research goes on, as Towi is being optimized to better accomplish the correlation with standarized test.
  • Institution(s): Areté Proyectos
  • Country: Mexico
  • Author(s): Rogelio Apiquian, Gamaliel Victoria, Marcos F. Rosetti, María F Gómez

Feasibility of towi as cognitive training video game for children

Finished: Sent to the International Journal of Child-Computer Interaction (Submission no: IJCCI_2019_9)

  • Description: The aim of the study was to evaluate the feasibility of using TOWI, as a cognitive training tool. Cognitive performance before and after playing TOWI was compared using standardized neuropsychological tools as well as cognitive screening games included as part of the TOWI platform. We found similar patterns of use in school and home as well as by boys and girls. Despite the exploratory nature of the current study, we found significant changes in several neuropsychological measures. Results are discussed in terms of the potential strengths that TOWI has as a training tool.
  • Institution(s): Institute of Biomedical Research, UNAM; National Institute of Psychiatry Ramon de la Fuente Muñiz; Kognisi; Areté Projects; University of the Americas
  • Country: Mexico
  • Author(s): Marcos F. Rosetti, Maria F. Gomez, Cesar Maya & Rogelio Apiquian.

Neuropsychological screening with towi: performance in 6- to 12-year-old children

Finished

  • Description: The aim of the study was to address developmental trends for basic cognitive functions such as attention, memory and executive functions from 6 to 12 years of age using the screening games on the TOWI platform. We tested 1139 participants. We compared and contrasted our results with similar studies and find similarities in the developmental trends, including the presence of gradual changes as well as quick “spurts” of improvement. We conclude that several performance variables of the tasks included in TOWI are sensitive to developmental changes, which in turn contributes to the face validity of TOWI as a screening tool
  • Institution(s): Institute of Biomedical Research, UNAM; National Institute of Psychiatry Ramon de la Fuente Muñiz; Kognisi; National Institute of Rehabilitation, Areté Projects; University of the Americas
  • Country: Mexico
  • Author(s): Marcos F. Rosetti, Maria F. Gomez, Marlene A. Galicia, Cesar Maya & Rogelio Apiquian.
